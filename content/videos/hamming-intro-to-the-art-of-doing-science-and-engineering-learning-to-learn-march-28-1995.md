# 🧠⚙️📐 Hamming Intro to The Art of Doing Science and Engineering Learning to Learn March 28 1995  
![Hamming, Intro to The Art of Doing Science and Engineering: Learning to Learn (March 28, 1995)](https://youtu.be/AD4b-52jtos)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
  
* 🎓 The purpose of this course is to prepare you for your technical future by focusing on style rather than technical content.  
* 🎨 Style is a matter of excellence that cannot be put into words but can be inferred through particular examples and coaching.  
* 🏃 Success is not a matter of copying a master; you must find a style that fits you and can cope with a changing future.  
* 🧗 Every generation faces harder tasks because they stand on the shoulders of those who did the easier problems first.  
* 🚜 Management often fails by assuming the methods that made them great are still appropriate for the next generation.  
* 🏫 Education is learning what, when, and why to do things, whereas training is simply learning how to do them.  
* 📉 Knowledge doubles roughly every seventeen years, meaning half of what you learn today will be obsolete in fifteen years.  
* 🧠 Learning to learn is the only viable strategy for a career that will span multiple technological revolutions.  
* 🧪 Science is the exploration of what you do not know, while engineering is the application of what you do know.  
* ⏳ The time between scientific discovery and engineering application is shrinking, requiring rapid adaptation.  
* 🔮 Long-term predictions are often pessimistic because people fail to believe in the power of geometric progression.  
* 🏛️ The past was less determined than historians claim, and the future is less open-ended than you might hope.  
* ⚖️ Future technology will be limited less by what is possible and more by social, legal, and ethical restraints.  
* ⚓ You must have a vision to make progress proportional to your effort; without a goal, you wander like a drunken sailor.  
* 🏗️ Computing will dominate the future because machines offer superior speed, accuracy, reliability, and freedom from boredom.  
* 🎖️ The good life is not composed of pleasant moments but is found in the struggle to achieve excellence and manage yourself.  
  
## 🤔 Evaluation  
  
* ⚖️ Richard Hamming emphasizes that individual style and the struggle for excellence define a great career, a view echoed in So Good They Can't Ignore You by Cal Newport, which argues that rare and valuable skills are built through deliberate practice rather than just following passion.  
* 📈 Hamming's observation on the exponential growth of knowledge aligns with the concept of the Law of Accelerating Returns described by Ray Kurzweil in The Singularity Is Near, published by Viking Press, which posits that technological change is exponential rather than linear.  
* 🛑 While Hamming suggests that social and legal restraints will be the primary bottleneck for future technology, [🤖📈 The Second Machine Age: Work, Progress, and Prosperity in a Time of Brilliant Technologies](../books/the-second-machine-age-work-progress-and-prosperity-in-a-time-of-brilliant-technologies.md) by Erik Brynjolfsson and Andrew McAfee, published by W. W. Norton & Company, suggests that the primary challenge is the lag in organizational and human adaptation to digital speed.  
* 🔬 Hamming distinguishes between science as exploration and engineering as application; however, The Pasteur's Quadrant by Donald E. Stokes, published by Brookings Institution Press, argues for a more integrated view where use-inspired basic research drives both fundamental understanding and practical use simultaneously.  
  
## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🧭 Q: Why is having a specific vision important for a technical career?  
  
⛵ A: Without a goal, effort results in a random walk where progress only grows by the square root of the steps taken, whereas a clear vision allows for progress that is directly proportional to the total effort expended.  
  
### 📚 Q: What is the difference between education and training?  
  
🛠️ A: Training focuses on the mechanics of how to perform a specific task, while education focuses on the higher-level understanding of what to do, when to do it, and why it is necessary.  
  
### 🔄 Q: How should a professional handle the rapid obsolescence of technical knowledge?  
  
💡 A: Because half of all technical knowledge becomes obsolete every fifteen years, professionals must prioritize learning to learn and mastering fundamentals rather than just clinging to specific current technologies.  
  
### 🤖 Q: Why will computers continue to replace human labor in complex systems?  
  
⚡ A: Computers provide massive advantages in speed, accuracy, and bandwidth, and unlike humans, they are immune to boredom, personal squabbles, and the need for recreation or pensions.  
  
## 📚 Book Recommendations  
  
### ↔️ Similar  
  
* 🧗 The Art of Doing Science and Engineering by Richard Hamming explores the mindset and habits required to perform world-class technical work.  
* 🏔️ Peak by Anders Ericsson describes how deliberate practice and mental models lead to the mastery and style Hamming advocates.  
  
### 🆚 Contrasting  
  
* 🎲 Range by David Epstein argues that generalists who jump between fields often excel more than those who specialize early in a single technical path.  
* 🌿 [🤏🧑 Small Is Beautiful: Economics as if People Mattered](../books/small-is-beautiful-economics-as-if-people-mattered.md) by E. F. Schumacher challenges the drive for constant exponential growth and technological dominance in favor of human-scale systems.  
  
### 🎨 Creatively Related  
  
* 🏛️ The Lessons of History by Will and Ariel Durant provides a broad overview of how human nature and social structures repeat patterns over millennia.  
* 🌊 Flow by Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi examines the psychology of the struggle and total immersion in a task that Hamming identifies as the core of a good life.
