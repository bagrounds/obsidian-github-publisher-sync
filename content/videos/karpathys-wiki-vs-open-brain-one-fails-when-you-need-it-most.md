# 🔒🆚🔓🚨 Karpathy's Wiki vs. Open Brain. One Fails When You Need It Most.  
![Karpathy's Wiki vs. Open Brain. One Fails When You Need It Most.](https://youtu.be/dxq7WtWxi44)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
* [🤖🧠💻 Andrej Karpathy](../people/andrej-karpathy.md) proposed a personal wiki system where AI acts as a writer to build and maintain structured notes in text files \[[00:00](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=0)].  
* 📝 This approach compiles knowledge once upon ingestion rather than spending compute to re-derive insights from scratch for every query \[[04:26](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=266)].  
* 📉 A major risk of the wiki model is editorial drift where AI makes synthesis decisions that might drop nuances or bake in errors over time \[[06:20](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=380)].  
* 🗄️ OpenBrain utilizes a query time system that stores raw data faithfully in structured tables and performs thinking only when a question is asked \[[09:22](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=562)].  
* 🏗️ Karpathy's wiki is ideal for solo research and deep conceptual connections but struggles with scale and precise operational queries \[[22:10](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=1330)].  
* 🔢 OpenBrain excels at handling high volumes of structured data, multi-agent access, and precise filtering across thousands of entries \[[22:40](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=1360)].  
* 🕰️ Neglected wikis can lead to active misinformation because they read confidently even when outdated, whereas databases simply show gaps \[[25:03](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=1503)].  
* 🔗 A hybrid architecture is proposed using OpenBrain as the durable source of truth with a generated wiki layer for human-readable summaries \[[30:31](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=1831)].  
* 🛠️ This transition moves the AI from a simple answer engine to a maintainer of thinking systems that support human judgment \[[37:37](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxq7WtWxi44&t=2257)].

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🤖 Q: What is the difference between write-time and query-time AI synthesis?  
🤖 A: Write-time synthesis like Karpathy's wiki processes and summarizes information as it enters the system to save on future compute, while query-time systems like OpenBrain wait until a question is asked to analyze raw data for maximum precision.  
  
### 📉 Q: Why might a personal AI wiki become unreliable over time?  
🤖 A: Because the AI makes editorial choices during synthesis, it can introduce subtle errors or ignore contradictions that become permanent fixtures of the notes, leading to a confident but inaccurate representation of reality.  
  
### 🏢 Q: Is a folder of text files suitable for corporate knowledge management?  
🤖 A: No, text-based wikis generally fail at corporate scale because they cannot handle complex relational queries, simultaneous multi-agent edits, or volumes exceeding 10,000 high-signal documents.
