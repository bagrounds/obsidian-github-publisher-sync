# 🌳🍎❓🛠️ Fruit Tree Care Troubleshooting  
![Fruit Tree Care Troubleshooting](https://youtu.be/qnhLUIPuk2Y)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
* 🔍 Inspect trees constantly to catch and correct small problems before they escalate into unmanageable infestations \[[01:17](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=77)].  
* 🎓 Access information from university .edu sites or Pacific Northwest sources to ensure guidance is regionally appropriate \[[01:42](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=102)].  
* 🪵 Recognize that 70 percent of plant issues stem from abiotic factors like soil compaction, mower damage, or weather stress \[[03:01](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=181)].  
* ☀️ Ensure trees receive at least eight hours of daily sun and are planted in well drained soil with a pH between six and seven \[[04:54](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=294)].  
* 🧪 Fertilize only if growth is poor, as over-fertilizing creates vigorous green growth that attracts aphids \[[06:13](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=373)].  
* 🌳 Prioritize dwarf rootstocks to keep trees at a reachable height for effective organic pest management and safety \[[08:00](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=480)].  
* 🍎 Thin fruit to one per cluster when they reach thumb size to prevent biennial bearing and reduce codling moth habitat \[[14:51](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=891)].  
* 🧼 Utilize organic options like spinosad or neem oil at night to protect pollinators while managing pests \[[20:50](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=1250)].  
* 🐛 Manage codling moths by wrapping trunks in corrugated cardboard or bagging individual fruit shortly after petal fall \[[24:05](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=1445)].  
* 🧹 Practice strict sanitation by removing all fallen fruit and leaves to eliminate overwintering sites for fungi and insects \[[31:20](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=1880)].

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🍎 Q: Why does my apple tree only produce fruit every other year?  
🍏 A: This is known as biennial bearing and usually occurs because the tree was not properly thinned during a heavy crop year, exhausting its resources \[[12:41](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=761)].  
  
### 🐝 Q: How can I spray my fruit trees without killing bees?  
🍯 A: Apply organic pesticides like neem oil or spinosad at dusk so they can dry overnight before pollinators become active in the morning \[[20:50](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=1250)].  
  
### 🐛 Q: What is the best way to stop worms in my apples?  
🪱 A: Use a combination of thinning fruit to prevent them from touching, bagging young fruit, or applying spinosad 10 days after petal fall \[[24:05](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=1445)].  
  
### 🍑 Q: Can I grow peaches successfully in a rainy climate?  
🌧️ A: Peaches are highly susceptible to leaf curl in wet winters; success is more likely if grown in pots and moved under cover from December to February \[[40:56](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qnhLUIPuk2Y&t=2456)].
