## ⚖️ Critical Evaluation  
  
* 🛡️ Ikenberry defends liberal internationalism as the best we can hope despite its inherent compromises, such as the entanglement of universalist values with great power hegemony.  
* 🤔 Critics note that Ikenberry’s argument sometimes exhibits a Fukuyaman leap of faith, suggesting the liberal order will inherently survive current challenges by adapting, possibly downplaying systemic issues.  
* ⚠️ While Ikenberry emphasizes internal threats like populism, some critiques argue he understates the role of unrestrained capitalism and issues like white supremacy as fundamental forces unraveling the liberal international system.  
* 📚 The book is seen as a formidable defense of the liberal international order, providing a long historical view that refutes the notion of liberal internationalism as merely a post-Cold War utopian project.  
* 🇺🇸 Some scholars contend that Ikenberry’s work, despite its academic rigor, serves American interests by framing global order through a U.S.-centric liberal West lens.  
* 🗣️ Ikenberry actively engages and responds to realist and revisionist-left criticisms, arguing that liberal internationalism is a pragmatic response to modernity's challenges rather than a utopian ideal.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 💡 Q: What is A World Safe for Democracy: Liberal Internationalism and the Crises of Global Order about?  
✅ A: G. John Ikenberry's book offers a comprehensive account of liberal internationalism, tracing its two-century history, analyzing its current crisis from both external illiberal challengers and internal nationalist-populist movements, and arguing for its reformed viability as the most effective project to protect liberal democracy in an interdependent world.  
  
### 💡 Q: What is liberal internationalism according to Ikenberry?  
✅ A: Ikenberry defines liberal internationalism as a tradition aiming to organize and reform the international order to strengthen and facilitate the security, welfare, and progress of liberal democracy. 🤝 It emphasizes international openness, ⚖️ multilateral rules-based institutions, 🤝 democratic solidarity, 🛡️ cooperative security, and 🌱 progressive social purposes.  
  
### 💡 Q: Did Woodrow Wilson succeed in making the world safe for democracy?  
✅ A: Woodrow Wilson's goal to make the world safe for democracy after WWI, while influential in shaping U.S. foreign policy, is widely considered to have failed in the short term, partly due to the power vacuum in Eastern Europe, the punitive nature of the Versailles Treaty, and the rise of later totalitarian regimes. 📜 Ikenberry interprets Wilson's slogan not as a call for military intervention to spread democracy, but as shaping the international environment through rules and norms for democracies to flourish.  
  
### 💡 Q: What are the main challenges to liberal internationalism today?  
✅ A: Liberal internationalism faces threats from external illiberal challengers like 🇨🇳 China and 🇷🇺 Russia, as well as internal nationalist-populist movements, economic inequality, and declining democratic norms within Western countries. 🇺🇸 There are also debates about whether it can persist without continued strong U.S. leadership.
