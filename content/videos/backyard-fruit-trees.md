# 🏡🌳🍎 Backyard Fruit Trees  
![Backyard Fruit Trees](https://youtu.be/NjdMiizUyG4)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
  
* 🍎 Evaluate the specific reasons for planting, such as food security, fruit quality, or teaching children, to determine if the long-term maintenance is truly desired \[[00:34](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=34)].  
* 🧬 Recognize that fruit trees belong to the Rosaceae family, sharing susceptibility to fungal diseases like black spot with common garden roses \[[02:50](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=170)].  
* 🍒 Avoid planting cherries and apricots west of the Cascades due to high humidity levels that foster rampant fungal infections and insect infestations \[[01:05:10](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=3910)].  
* 🐛 Combat the cherry fruit fly and spotted wing drosophila by netting trees and maintaining rigorous spray schedules to prevent larvae in the fruit \[[09:56](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=596)].  
* 🍑 Manage peach leaf curl and brown rot through heavy annual pruning and dormant copper sprays applied during the winter months \[[16:47](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=1007)].  
* 🍐 Select asian pears for a lower-maintenance option as they are generally easier to grow and produce more consistent crops than european varieties \[[27:44](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=1664)].  
* 🌳 Prioritize M9 or B9 dwarfing rootstocks for apples to ensure trees remain at a manageable height for pruning, thinning, and harvesting \[[38:31](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=2311)].  
* ✂️ Perform annual pruning to maintain a 45 to 60 degree branch angle, which optimizes fruit production and structural integrity \[[41:38](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=2498)].  
* 🤏 Thin fruit clusters in June to a single apple per bunch to prevent biennial bearing and reduce hiding spots for pests \[[52:19](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=3139)].  
* 💧 Deliver five gallons of water weekly using a punctured bucket system to ensure deep root penetration without wetting the foliage \[[47:06](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=2826)].  
* 🧤 Practice strict sanitation by removing fallen fruit and disinfecting pruning tools with Lysol or alcohol between different trees \[[01:00:17](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=3617)].  
* 🛡️ Protect tree trunks from girdling by rabbits and mice using 18-inch tall wire mesh cages maintained for the life of the tree \[[01:01:44](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=3704)].  
* 🌿 Consider figs or quince as hardy, ornamental alternatives that require significantly less intervention than traditional stone or pome fruits \[[01:04:13](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=3853)].

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🪣 Q: How can I ensure my young fruit trees receive the correct amount of water?  
💧 A: Place a five-gallon bucket with several small holes drilled near the bottom next to the tree trunk and fill it once per week so the water slowly trickles into the root zone \[[01:15:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=4502)].  
  
### 🌳 Q: What is the difference between a standard and a dwarf apple tree?  
📏 A: Standard trees grow too large for easy maintenance, while dwarf rootstocks like M9 keep trees small enough to manage from the ground or a short ladder \[[38:31](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=2311)].  
  
### 🦠 Q: How do I prevent diseases from spreading between my different fruit trees?  
🧼 A: Sanitize your pruning shears with a disinfectant spray like Lysol or a weak bleach solution every time you move from one tree to the next \[[01:17:09](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=4629)].  
  
### 🐛 Q: Is there a way to protect apples from worms without using heavy pesticides?  
🛍️ A: You can tie small mesh or nylon bags around individual fruits shortly after thinning to create a physical barrier against egg-laying insects \[[54:34](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NjdMiizUyG4&t=3274)].
