## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### ❓ What is the rationale for demanding public ownership of major artificial intelligence companies?  
  
💡 The foundation of modern artificial intelligence relies on the cumulative labor, intellectual production, and data provided by the public over generations, including scientific work, artistic contributions, and personal interactions; therefore, the public should possess a significant ownership stake to prevent private interests from monopolizing the benefits and to ensure the technology serves collective human needs.  
  
### ❓ How would public representation on boards of directors function to protect the workforce?  
  
💡 By placing public-appointed members on the boards of major AI corporations, the public gains the necessary power to veto or block decisions explicitly designed to maximize corporate profits at the expense of mass employment, privacy, or mental health, ensuring that human welfare is prioritized over unfettered technological acceleration.  
  
### ❓ Why does the current healthcare system in the United States persist despite high costs and poor outcomes?  
  
💡 The American healthcare system is designed to prioritize the financial interests of insurance companies and pharmaceutical corporations, which generate billions in profits, rather than providing healthcare as a universal human right as achieved in other developed nations.  
  
### ❓ What is the significance of the grassroots political strategy compared to traditional campaign methods?  
  
💡 While traditional political campaigns rely heavily on corporate funding, professional consultants, and poll-tested advertisements, a grassroots approach focuses on direct engagement, door-to-door organizing, and mobilizing working-class individuals to build a political movement capable of overcoming the influence of billionaire-funded special interests.
