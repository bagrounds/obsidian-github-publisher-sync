## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🤏 Q: How do G and Q relate to each other in American Sign Language?  
  
🖐️ A: Q is simply the G handshape pointed toward the ground instead of held sideways. \[[05:54](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DBQINq0SsAw&t=354)]  
  
### ✌️ Q: What is the difference between the letters K and P in American Sign Language?  
  
🖐️ A: Both letters use the same handshape with the thumb between two fingers, but K is held upright and P is pointed down. \[[05:43](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DBQINq0SsAw&t=343)]  
  
### 🍎 Q: How can a beginner distinguish the letter A from the letter S in American Sign Language?  
  
🖐️ A: For A the thumb stays at the side of the index finger, whereas for S the thumb moves to the front of the fingers. \[[06:17](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DBQINq0SsAw&t=377)]  
  
### ✍️ Q: Which letters in the ASL alphabet involve moving the hand?  
  
🖐️ A: The letters J and Z are the only two letters that require tracing their shapes in the air. \[[08:16](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DBQINq0SsAw&t=496)]

* 📗 The Joy of Signing by Lottie L Riekehof from Gospel Publishing House offers a foundational approach to learning the manual alphabet.

* 📕 Signs of Language by Edward Klima and Ursula Bellugi from Harvard University Press examines the biological and linguistic structures of ASL.
