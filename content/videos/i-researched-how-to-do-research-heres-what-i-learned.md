# 🔍🔬🧠 I Researched How To Do Research, Here's What I Learned  
![I Researched How To Do Research, Here's What I Learned](https://youtu.be/Pz5Nq8PDKDo)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
  
* 🎯 Clarify the specific question or goal before starting to avoid aimless clicking and falling into bias bubbles.  
* 🧠 Acknowledge personal biases and subjective viewpoints to prevent cherrypicking resources that only confirm existing beliefs.  
* 🎓 Recognize when a topic requires expert knowledge and prioritize sources with firsthand experience or long-term professional background.  
* 🕸️ Utilize the spiderweb method by starting with broad searches and following citations deeper into primary articles and studies.  
* 🔍 Master boolean operators like AND, OR, and NOT to refine search results and find exact phrases with quotation marks.  
* 🛑 Implement the SIFT method: stop to think, investigate the source, find better coverage, and trace claims to the original context.  
* 🚩 View extreme certainty as a red flag because true experts often emphasize nuance, questions, and the evolving nature of the scientific method.  
* 🧪 Distinguish between quantitative data-driven studies and qualitative vibe-based research to evaluate the strength of evidence.  
* 🤝 Be honest about the limitations of your findings and avoid the temptation to wrap conclusions in a perfect, misleading bow.  
  
## 🤔 Evaluation  
  
🛡️ The speaker promotes the SIFT method, which aligns with the Stanford History Education Group's research on lateral reading as the most effective way to verify digital information. 🌐 While the video suggests starting with Wikipedia for a broad overview, the Harvard College Writing Program emphasizes that while Wikipedia is useful for initial orientation, it should never be a final source for academic work due to its crowdsourced nature. ⚖️ To gain a deeper understanding of information literacy, one should explore the concept of echo chambers as detailed in the book Networked Publics by the MIT Press, which explains how algorithms mathematically reinforce the biases the speaker warns against.  
  
## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🕵️ Q: What is the SIFT method for evaluating online information?  
🕵️ A: SIFT is an acronym for four moves used to verify facts: Stop, Investigate the source, Find better coverage, and Trace claims back to the original context.  
  
### ➕ Q: How do boolean operators improve digital research results?  
➕ A: Boolean operators like AND, OR, and NOT allow researchers to combine or exclude specific keywords to filter out irrelevant data and narrow results.  
  
### 🧪 Q: Why should a researcher be wary of highly certain claims?  
🧪 A: High certainty often indicates bias or a lack of nuance, whereas credible scientific research usually acknowledges limitations and the possibility of new evidence.  
  
### 🕷️ Q: What does the spiderweb method of research involve?  
🕷️ A: The spiderweb method involves starting with a general source and following its citations and references to discover more specific, primary documents and related data.
