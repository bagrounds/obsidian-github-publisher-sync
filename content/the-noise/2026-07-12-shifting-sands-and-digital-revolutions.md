## ⚔️ Geopolitical Frontlines and Escalating Tensions  
  
💔 Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy is holding officials accountable for a strike this week on a weapons warehouse in Vyshneve, outside Kyiv, which caused secondary explosions and killed 10 people in a residential area, according to a Saturday report by The Guardian. 💥 Russian missile and drone attacks on Ukraine continued on Saturday, killing eight people and wounding dozens more, with glide bombs hitting Sumy and Zaporizhzhia, The Guardian also reported. 🗣️ Separately, a Ukrainian brigade spokesperson noted Russian forces are increasing drone strikes around Kramatorsk, aiming to encircle the city rather than assaulting it directly, as reported Saturday by the Institute for the Study of War (ISW). 🇷🇺 The Kremlin reiterated on Friday that Ukraine's continued drone and missile attacks inside Russia justify widening a "buffer zone" in eastern Ukraine, rejecting diplomatic negotiations, The Moscow Times reported.  
  
🇮🇷 The US and Iran continued their exchange of military strikes this week, with Iran claiming to have attacked US bases in Kuwait, Bahrain, and northern Jordan in retaliation for US strikes on Iranian cities, as reported Friday by Al Jazeera and MR Online. 📞 US President Donald Trump stated on Friday that the ceasefire with Iran was "over" but that the US had agreed to Iran's request for peace talks to continue, according to reports from The Jerusalem Post and ETV Bharat. 🚨 Israel reportedly shared intelligence with the US this week about a new Iranian plot to assassinate President Trump, ABS-CBN News reported on Thursday. 🛡️ Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz warned on Thursday that Israel is prepared to resume military operations against Iran with "even greater force" if necessary, Gulf News reported.  
  
## 💔 Humanitarian Crises and Persistent Suffering  
  
🇻🇪 The death toll from Venezuela's twin earthquakes on June 24 has tragically risen to 4,333, with 16,740 injured and approximately 17,907 people left homeless, according to a Saturday report from ReliefWeb and The Guardian. 🌍 The United Nations has launched an urgent appeal for nearly $300 million to scale up earthquake relief operations, aiming to assist 1.3 million people over the next six months, ReliefWeb stated. 🦺 While search operations continue in some uncertain sites for survivors, the focus is shifting to recovery, with the Venezuelan government announcing the distribution of housing to affected individuals beginning next week, GV Wire reported.  
  
🦠 The Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo has claimed 600 lives, making it the fastest-growing Ebola outbreak ever, with 1,759 confirmed cases, France 24 reported on Friday. 💉 Two other people have died in neighboring Uganda, which has 20 confirmed cases, Go Local Prov reported.  
  
## 💰 Economic Currents and Fiscal Forecasts  
  
📉 The International Monetary Fund (IMF) projects global growth to slow to 3.0 percent in 2026, slightly down from previous forecasts, citing the impact of the Middle East war partly offset by accelerated demand from the global technology cycle, according to its July 2026 World Economic Outlook Update. 📈 Global headline inflation is expected to increase from 4.1 percent in 2025 to 4.7 percent in 2026, driven mainly by higher energy and food prices and the effects of the AI buildout on consumer prices, the IMF and Raymond James reported.  
  
🇺🇸 A housing affordability bill became law Friday without President Trump's signature, as he withheld approval in protest of Congress not passing a strict voter ID bill, The Vindicator reported. 🏛️ Trump also ousted members of the federal Election Assistance Commission, a move aimed at expanding White House influence over how US elections are conducted, according to The Vindicator and PBS. ✈️ Separately, the airport in West Palm Beach, Florida, has been officially renamed President Donald J. Trump International Airport, making it the first airport named after a sitting US president, CNBC reported on Friday.  
  
## 🚀 AI's Rapid Evolution and Scientific Horizons  
  
🤖 Artificial intelligence continues its rapid evolution, with OpenAI publicly launching its GPT-5.6 family (Sol, Terra, and Luna) on Thursday, claiming Sol sets a new coding benchmark and is 54% more token efficient on AI coding tasks, NeuralBuddies reported. 💬 OpenAI also unveiled GPT-Live, a new generation of real-time conversational voice AI capable of listening, speaking, and reasoning simultaneously, according to Medium and Mark McNeilly. 🏛️ Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 315 on July 6, making Illinois the first US state to enact frontier-model-specific AI safety requirements, which include transparency obligations and catastrophic-risk assessment, Unrot.co reported.  
  
🌍 Google has launched the Google Africa Applied AI Lab in Accra, an initiative to support African researchers and entrepreneurs with early access to AI technologies and technical guidance, Medium reported. 💼 Accenture Edge and Google Cloud are partnering to bring a suite of pre-built agentic AI solutions to mid-market companies, aimed at areas like customer intelligence, cybersecurity, and business operations, Solutions Review reported. 💻 Meta unveiled Muse Spark 1.1, an AI model specializing in autonomous agents for software development and advanced tool use, designed to make powerful AI agents more accessible, Medium reported. 🧠 Anthropic has launched Claude Corps, a paid 12-month fellowship program to train future AI professionals within nonprofit organizations, with no degree required, according to Medium.  
  
🔋 Researchers have reportedly solved the mystery of how soft lithium dendrites crack the hard ceramic in solid-state batteries, a breakthrough that could lead to safer, longer-lasting batteries for electric vehicles and smartphones, ScienceDaily reported on Friday. 🌌 Scientists have also made an important step toward building quantum detectors that could reveal dark matter and ancient gravitational waves, utilizing a prototype device with two clouds of ultracold atoms, ScienceDaily reported.  
  
## 🥵 Climate's Growing Fury and Environmental Warnings  
  
🌡️ Europe continues to experience deadly heatwaves, with over 2,700 deaths in France and more than 5,000 "excess deaths" in Germany attributed to the June heatwave, according to analysis covered by Carbon Brief. 🇺🇸 Most of the continental United States is bracing for an "unusually large, strong and long-lasting heat dome" this weekend and next week, with temperatures 15 to 25 degrees Fahrenheit warmer than normal, The Washington Post reported on Friday. 🌊 The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) forecasters said Thursday there is an 81% chance of a "very strong" El Niño between October and December, which could make it one of the largest since 1950, raising concerns about amplified impacts from human-driven climate change, Gizmodo reported.  
  
🇨🇳 Scientists warn that climate change and the El Niño phenomenon are exposing China to "catastrophic storms" that will test its resilience in 2026, with the nation's latest official climate report finding that "extreme weather and climate events...have become more frequent and severe," Reuters and China National Radio reported on Friday. 🌳 China has published an "action plan" for peaking emissions by 2030, with targets including new energy vehicles making up 30% of cars on the road, Xinhua reported.
