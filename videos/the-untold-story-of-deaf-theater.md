* 🏛️ The video focuses on the 1988 protest at Gallaudet University, which is recognized by the National Association of the Deaf as a landmark civil rights victory that fundamentally changed public perception of deafness.  
* 🗣️ While the film emphasizes artistic expression, high-quality sources like the book Forbidden Signs by Douglas C. Baynton from the University of Chicago Press detail the historical struggle against oralism (forcing Deaf people to speak) which adds depth to the actors' resistance.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🤟 Q: What is the primary goal of Deaf theater?  
🤟 A: It aims to provide full accessibility for all audiences through sign language while showcasing the unique culture and history of the Deaf community \[[03:58](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LTUgZ2J47vw&t=238)].  
  
### 🏫 Q: What was the significance of the Deaf President Now protest?  
🏫 A: It was a movement where students demanded their identity be represented in their leadership after a non-signing individual was appointed president \[[01:53](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LTUgZ2J47vw&t=113)].  
  
### 🥘 Q: What does the term dinner table syndrome refer to?  
🥘 A: It describes the feeling of being left out of conversations when Hearing people talk around a Deaf person and refuse to interpret in real-time \[[11:23](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LTUgZ2J47vw&t=683)].  
  
### 🎬 Q: How do Deaf actors translate scripts into sign language?  
🎬 A: Actors work with a director of artistic sign language to translate English text into visual movements including eyes, mouth, and eyebrows \[[05:39](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LTUgZ2J47vw&t=339)].

### 🆚 Contrasting

* 📙 The Mask of Benevolence by Harlan Lane argues that the Hearing establishment often controls and pathologizes the Deaf experience under the guise of helping.
