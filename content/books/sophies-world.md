* 📖 **Meta-Narrative:** Story-within-a-story device; characters discovering their fictional nature.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 💡 Q: What is Sophie's World about?  
✅ A: Sophie's World is a novel about a teenage girl, Sophie Amundsen, who receives a mysterious correspondence course in philosophy, taking her on a journey through Western thought while uncovering a deeper mystery about her own existence and the nature of reality.  
  
### 💡 Q: Who wrote Sophie's World?  
✅ A: Sophie's World was written by Norwegian author Jostein Gaarder.  
  
### 💡 Q: What philosophical concepts are covered in Sophie's World?  
✅ A: Sophie's World covers a wide range of philosophical concepts, including the ideas of pre-Socratics, Socrates, Plato, Aristotle, medieval thinkers like Augustine and Aquinas, Renaissance thought, Enlightenment philosophers (Descartes, Locke, Hume, Kant), and 19th and 20th-century movements like Romanticism and Existentialism.  
  
### 💡 Q: Is Sophie's World suitable for beginners in philosophy?  
✅ A: Yes, Sophie's World is widely regarded as an excellent introduction to philosophy for beginners and young adults due to its engaging narrative and accessible explanations of complex ideas.  
  
### 💡 Q: What is the main theme of Sophie's World?  
✅ A: The main themes of Sophie's World include the exploration of the history of philosophy, the questioning of human existence and reality, the nature of free will, and the importance of cultivating a sense of wonder.  
  
### 💡 Q: Is Sophie's World a true story?  
✅ A: No, Sophie's World is a fictional novel, though it extensively covers the factual history of Western philosophy through its narrative.
