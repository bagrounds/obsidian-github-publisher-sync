## 🤖 AI Summary  
  
* 👮 Police use multifaceted surveillance gear to identify and track protesters in real time.  
* 🤳 Facial recognition apps like Clearview AI match photos against billions of internet images.  
* 🎭 Algorithms now identify individuals even if they wear masks or obscure their faces.  
* 📱 Cell site simulators or IMSI catchers impersonate towers to grab unique phone IDs.  
* 🗺️ Geofence warrants allow authorities to pull location data for everyone in a specific area.  
* 🕵️ Data brokers sell detailed profiles including browsing history and purchase records to police.  
* 🚗 License plate readers track vehicle movement and can even capture text on clothing or signs.  
* 🛸 Drones provide cheap aerial surveillance and can carry sophisticated sensors or thermal cameras.  
* 🚶 Emerging tech like gait recognition identifies people by their unique walking patterns.  
* 📉 Minimize risk by leaving phones at home or using Faraday bags to block signals.  
* 🔐 Disable biometrics like FaceID to prevent forced phone unlocking during detentions.  
* 💬 Use end to end encrypted messaging apps such as Signal for private communications.  
  
## 🤔 Evaluation  
  
* ⚖️ Wired focuses on privacy risks while the International Association of Chiefs of Police argues these tools are essential for public safety and identifying violent actors.  
* 🛡️ Organizations like the Electronic Frontier Foundation highlight that these technologies disproportionately target marginalized communities and chill free speech.  
* 🌍 Investigating the Global Expansion of AI Surveillance by the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace provides a broader look at how these systems are exported globally.  
  
## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🕵️ Q: How do stingrays find people at protests?  
  
🚨 A: These devices act as fake cell towers and trick phones into connecting so they can capture unique identification numbers.  
  
### 🎭 Q: Can facial recognition identify me if I wear a mask?  
  
🤖 A: Modern systems use facial geometry and can often identify individuals based on the areas around the eyes and forehead.  
  
### 📍 Q: What is a geofence warrant?  
  
📂 A: It is a legal request for tech companies to provide data on every device that was present within a specific geographic boundary.  
  
### 📱 Q: Why should I turn off FaceID at a protest?  
  
🔒 A: It prevents law enforcement from unlocking your phone by simply holding it up to your face without your consent.
