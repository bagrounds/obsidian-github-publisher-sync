## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### ❓ How does fatherhood change the male brain?  
  
The transition to fatherhood induces an adaptive remodeling of the brain, marked by a reduction in gray matter volume. This process streamlines neural networks, enhancing efficiency in areas associated with social cognition and the ability to mentalize or understand the perspectives of others.  
  
### ❓ Why do testosterone levels drop in new fathers?  
  
The decline in testosterone following the arrival of a child marks a shift in reproductive strategy. While high testosterone levels support competition and mating-seeking behaviors, reduced levels allow fathers to prioritize the quality of care and investment in their existing offspring, thereby improving survival outcomes.  
  
### ❓ Does paternity leave influence fatherhood involvement?  
  
Generous and universal paternity leave policies, especially those that include specific incentives for fathers, significantly encourage and normalize hands-on caregiving. These policies reduce workplace stigma and empower men to spend more time actively parenting, which supports better outcomes for children and partners.
