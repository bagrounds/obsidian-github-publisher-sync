✅ A: Source Code: My Beginnings is a memoir by Bill Gates that chronicles his early life, including his childhood in Seattle, family influences, significant friendships, his discovery of computers and coding, and the foundational period of Microsoft up to the late 1970s.  
  
### 💡 Q: Who is the author of Source Code: My Beginnings?  
✅ A: The author of Source Code: My Beginnings is Bill Gates, co-founder of Microsoft, technologist, business leader, and philanthropist.  
  
### 💡 Q: What period of Bill Gates's life does Source Code: My Beginnings cover?  
✅ A: Source Code: My Beginnings covers Bill Gates's formative years, from his childhood in Seattle through his early teens, college, and the beginning of Microsoft, concluding in the late 1970s when Microsoft signed its first deal with Apple.  
  
### 💡 Q: What are the main themes in Source Code: My Beginnings?  
✅ A: Key themes in Source Code: My Beginnings include independence and self-discovery, intellectual curiosity and passion, the significant influence of family and friendships, innovation and entrepreneurship, and personal growth. The book also explores his struggles to fit in and his early experiences with coding and computers.  
  
### 💡 Q: Is Source Code: My Beginnings focused on Microsoft's history?  
✅ A: While Source Code: My Beginnings touches upon the foundational years of Microsoft, it is primarily a personal story about how Bill Gates became who he is, focusing on his childhood, early passions, and pursuits rather than a detailed history of the company or future technology.
