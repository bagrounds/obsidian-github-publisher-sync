# 🍏🌳✅✂️ Fruit Tree selection and pruning  
![Fruit Tree selection and pruning](https://youtu.be/twfzPEuDjOk)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
* 🌳 Select rootstock carefully as it determines the ultimate height, cold hardiness, and pest resistance of the fruit tree \[[10:35](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=635)].  
* 📏 Choose specific apple rootstocks like M27 for containers (6 feet) or M7 for semi-dwarf trees (16-19 feet) to avoid unmanageable growth \[[13:08](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=788)].  
* 🐝 Ensure proper pollination by planting compatible varieties, as many fruit trees are not self-fruitful and require cross-pollination from bees or wind \[[16:41](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=1001)].  
* ☀️ Prioritize site selection with full sun, good air circulation, and well-drained soil to prevent disease and ensure vigorous growth \[[21:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=1262)].  
* 🚫 Never bury the graft union when planting; keeping the bump above the soil line prevents the scion from growing its own roots and bypassing rootstock benefits \[[25:34](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=1534)].  
* ✂️ Apply the four Ds of pruning by removing damaged, dead, diseased, and dysfunctional wood such as suckers and water sprouts \[[31:12](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=1872)].  
* 🪚 Utilize thinning cuts to remove entire branches back to their origin, which improves light penetration and air circulation \[[39:16](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=2356)].  
* 🛑 Avoid heading cuts that merely shorten branches, as these stimulate bushy growth and decrease overall fruit quality \[[41:48](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=2508)].  
* ❌ Refrain from topping trees because it creates a maintenance nightmare, increases liability, and destroys the tree's natural structure \[[46:21](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=2781)].  
* 🍎 Practice fruit thinning to improve the size and quality of the remaining crop and ensure the tree produces fruit the following year \[[53:07](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=3187)].  
* 🧪 Monitor tree health through soil testing every two to three years and only fertilize if annual growth is less than twelve inches \[[51:17](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=3077)].

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 📏 Q: How do I know which rootstock is best for a small backyard?  
📏 A: Use dwarfing rootstocks like M9 or M27 for apples which keep trees under ten feet, making them easier to harvest and prune without ladders \[[13:40](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=820)].  
  
### ✂️ Q: What is the difference between a thinning cut and a heading cut?  
✂️ A: A thinning cut removes a branch completely at its point of origin to open the canopy, while a heading cut removes only the tip, causing the tree to become overly bushy \[[39:16](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=2356)].  
  
### 🍎 Q: Why should I remove perfectly good baby fruit from my tree?  
🍎 A: Thinning fruit prevents branches from breaking under weight and ensures the remaining fruit grows larger and develops better flavor \[[53:07](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=3187)].  
  
### 🚫 Q: Is it okay to cut the top off a fruit tree that has grown too tall?  
🚫 A: No, topping is not a justifiable practice because it triggers a flush of weak, vertical shoots and increases the risk of disease and decay \[[46:11](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=twfzPEuDjOk&t=2771)].
