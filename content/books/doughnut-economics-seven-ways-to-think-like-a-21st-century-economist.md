## 🤖 AI Summary  
  
### 🍩 Core Philosophy: The Doughnut  
* 📝 **Definition**: A visual framework for sustainable development, balancing human needs with planetary limits.  
* 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 **Inner Ring (Social Foundation)**: Minimum requirements for human well-being (e.g., food, water, education, health, equity, political voice) derived from UN Sustainable Development Goals.  
* ⛰️ **Outer Ring (Ecological Ceiling)**: Nine planetary boundaries (e.g., climate change, biodiversity loss, ocean acidification) not to be overshot.  
* ✅ **Safe and Just Space**: The area between the rings where humanity can thrive sustainably.  
  
### 🧠 Seven Ways to Think Like a 21st-Century Economist  
* 🎯 **1. Change the Goal**: From GDP growth to thriving in the Doughnut.  
* 🖼️ **2. See the Big Picture**: Economy embedded in society and the living world.  
* 🌱 **3. Nurture Human Nature**: From rational economic man to social, adaptable, interdependent beings.  
* ⚙️ **4. Get Savvy with Systems**: Recognize dynamic complexity, move beyond mechanical equilibrium.  
* 🤲 **5. Design to Distribute**: From trickle-down to distributive by design (e.g., wealth, land, knowledge, technology).  
* ♻️ **6. Create to Regenerate**: From grow now, clean up later to regenerative by design (e.g., circular economy, renewable energy).  
* ⚖️ **7. Be Agnostic about Growth**: Acknowledge that nothing grows forever; aim for thriving, not endless growth.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 💡 Q: What is the core idea behind Doughnut Economics: Seven Ways to Think Like a 21st-Century Economist?  
✅ A: Doughnut Economics proposes an economic model that enables humanity to thrive by meeting everyone's essential needs (social foundation) without overshooting the planet's ecological limits (ecological ceiling).  
  
### 💡 Q: Who wrote Doughnut Economics: Seven Ways to Think Like a 21st-Century Economist?  
✅ A: The book was written by Kate Raworth, an Oxford economist and co-founder of the Doughnut Economics Action Lab.  
  
### 💡 Q: What are the seven ways Kate Raworth suggests for rethinking economics?  
✅ A: Kate Raworth proposes changing the goal from GDP to the Doughnut, seeing the big picture, nurturing human nature, getting savvy with systems, designing to distribute, creating to regenerate, and being agnostic about growth.  
  
### 💡 Q: Has Doughnut Economics: Seven Ways to Think Like a 21st-Century Economist been implemented in practice?  
✅ A: Yes, cities like Amsterdam have adopted the Doughnut model as a framework for their urban development strategies, aiming to balance social well-being and ecological sustainability.  
  
### 💡 Q: How does Doughnut Economics address environmental concerns?  
✅ A: Doughnut Economics places Earth's ecological limits (planetary boundaries) as an outer ring, emphasizing the need for economies to operate regeneratively and distributively to avoid climate change, biodiversity loss, and other environmental degradation.
