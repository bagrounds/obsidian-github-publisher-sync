### 🏆 Paige Watson’s ADHD & Software Development: The Cheat Sheet  
  
#### 🧠 Core Philosophy: ADHD as Focus Management  
  
* 🎯 **Focus Control:** ADHD is not a lack of focus, but an inability to control its direction. \[[06:01](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=361)]  
* 🌊 **Hyperfocus Risks:** High-intensity flow can lead to cold coffee syndrome (neglecting physical needs like food/hydration). \[[06:19](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=379)]  
* 🧊 **The Freeze:** Large, ambiguous tasks trigger overwhelm and executive function shutdown. \[[07:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=422)]  
* 💾 **Memory Buffer:** Short-term memory flushes easily; important tasks often fail to write to the hard drive. \[[10:32](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=632)]  
  
#### 🤝 Collaborative Programming & Guardrails  
  
* 👥 **Mob/Ensemble Programming:** Full-team collaboration provides external structure and prevents rabbit holes. \[[08:00](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=480)]  
* ⌨️ **Smart Input:** While driving (typing), the ADHD brain can focus on implementation while the team navigates. \[[12:14](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=734)]  
* 🚧 **Verbal Guardrails:** Teammates act as external checks to pull developers back from over-engineering or irrelevant refactors. \[[12:43](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=763)]  
* 👯 **Body Doubling:** Working in the same physical or virtual space as another person to maintain task adherence. \[[19:15](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=1155)]  
  
#### 🧪 Technical Practices for Dopamine & Structure  
  
* 🧪 **Test-Driven Development (TDD):** Converts requirements into small, actionable code targets. \[[08:08](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=488)]  
* 💊 **Dopamine Loops:** Frequent passing tests provide small, consistent rewards that maintain engagement. \[[08:29](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=509)]  
* 📝 **Code as Requirements:** TDD ensures memory lapses don't result in missing test cases or forgotten logic. \[[11:51](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=711)]  
* ✂️ **Tiny Chunks:** Break work into the smallest possible units to prevent cognitive overload. \[[11:42](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=702)]  
  
#### 🌳 Visualization via Discovery Trees  
  
* 🌲 **Just-In-Time Design:** Visual task breakdown performed at the last responsible moment rather than upfront. \[[23:37](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=1417)]  
* 📍 **Visual Status:** Use markers (ticks for in-progress, slashes for done) to see progress instantly. \[[24:52](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=1492)]  
* 🛤️ **The Roadmap:** Maintain a high-level direction but only detail the next 2-4 hours of work. \[[26:07](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=1567)]  
* 📢 **Information Radiator:** The tree serves as a transparent status update for management without needing meetings. \[[27:01](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=1621)]  
  
#### 🏢 Psychological Safety & Team Dynamics  
  
* 🔓 **Self-Disclosure:** Vulnerability about needs allows the team to accommodate energy fluctuations. \[[18:52](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=1132)]  
* 🔋 **Recharge Breaks:** Normalize quiet time or stepping away from the mob to manage sensory or social exhaustion. \[[20:41](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=1241)]  
* 🛠️ **Experimentation:** Treat workflows as code - test, iterate, and discard processes that don't fit the human element. \[[30:23](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=69aVBkPYwKs&t=1823)]

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🌲 Q: What are discovery trees in software development?  
  
🌲 A: Discovery trees are visual maps used to break down complex features into smaller, manageable tasks just in time, helping teams avoid over-designing and reducing cognitive overwhelm.  
  
### 👥 Q: How does mob programming help developers?  
  
👥 A: Mob programming acts as a social and professional guardrail where teammates help maintain focus on the immediate task, preventing the individual from getting lost in refactoring rabbit holes.  
  
### 🧪 Q: Why is Test Driven Development effective for neurodivergent brains?  
  
🧪 A: Test Driven Development breaks work into tiny, binary success states that provide frequent feedback and dopamine hits, which helps manage the executive function challenges associated with long-term tasks.
