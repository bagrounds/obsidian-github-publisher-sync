## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### ❓ Q: What is the 60-day deadline mentioned regarding Iran?  
  
🗳️ A: This refers to the War Powers Resolution which requires a president to terminate any use of United States Armed Forces within 60 days unless Congress has declared war or provided specific authorization.  
  
### 🗳️ Q: How does a president legally avoid calling a conflict a war?  
  
🛡️ A: Administrations often classify actions as limited military operations or kinetic strikes to argue that the scale of involvement does not trigger the formal requirements of the War Powers Act.  
  
### 📜 Q: What does the 22nd Amendment of the Constitution establish?  
  
🚫 A: The 22nd Amendment explicitly states that no person shall be elected to the office of the President more than twice, effectively setting a two-term limit for the executive.  
  
### 💂 Q: Why would a British monarch speak to the United States Congress?  
  
🤝 A: While rare, such addresses are typically diplomatic gestures meant to reinforce shared values, though they can be interpreted as a commentary on the state of democratic institutions.
