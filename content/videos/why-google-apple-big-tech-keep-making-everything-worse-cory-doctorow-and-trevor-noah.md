# 📉🏢📉 Why Google, Apple & Big Tech Keep Making Everything Worse | Cory Doctorow and Trevor Noah  
![Why Google, Apple & Big Tech Keep Making Everything Worse | Cory Doctorow and Trevor Noah](https://youtu.be/CK8M5sKWRkc)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
  
* 🚀 Corporations use restrictive Digital Rights Management (DRM) to remotely downgrade products, rendering consumer purchases unusable or unreliable.  
* 🔒 Anti-circumvention laws, specifically the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) 1201, empower companies to legally block attempts to repair or modify owned hardware.  
* 🚄 Manufacturers utilize predatory maintenance models where products are sold cheaply, then booby-trapped to force exorbitant repair fees through proprietary restrictions.  
* ⚖️ The failure to enforce antitrust laws, such as the Robinson-Patman Act, allowed major retailers like Walmart to demand preferential discounts.  
* 💰 These exclusive pricing deals force wholesalers to increase prices for all other retailers, effectively crushing smaller competitors and inflating market costs.  
* 📈 Monopolistic grocers and food producers actively manipulate market pricing, leading to widespread inflation in consumer food prices.  
* 🌐 The modern internet landscape evolved from military and academic roots into a corporate-controlled system designed to maximize shareholder value at the expense of user autonomy.  
  
## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🧩 Q: What mechanism allows corporations to render purchased digital products unusable?  
  
A: Companies utilize Digital Rights Management (DRM) combined with restrictive terms of service to remotely disable or modify software, games, and hardware functionality after the point of sale.  
  
### 📜 Q: How did anti-circumvention laws originate?  
  
A: These laws were driven by corporate policy experts within the Clinton administration who bypassed congressional oversight by advocating for specific language at the United Nations World Intellectual Property Organization.  
  
### 🛒 Q: Why have grocery prices increased according to the discussion?  
  
A: Large retail chains leverage preferential wholesale discounts that force suppliers to raise prices for smaller competitors, thereby artificially inflating costs across the entire market.
