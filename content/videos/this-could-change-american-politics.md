## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🧣 Q: What is the historical origin of the term redneck?  
🧣 A: The term refers to striking coal miners of various races during the 1921 Blair Mountain uprising who wore red bandanas around their necks to signify their solidarity against corporate and government exploitation.  
  
### 🏗️ Q: How can a movement take over an existing political party?  
🏗️ A: Mass movements can inhabit a viable party by organizing from the bottom up and installing representatives who prioritize the movement's goals over the party establishment, similar to how Donald Trump transformed the Republican Party.  
  
### 🏘️ Q: Why is local organizing considered more effective than national campaigning?  
🏘️ A: Local organizing builds political power through community relationships and solving material issues, which creates a foundation of trust that can eventually reverse systemic failures seen on the national news.
