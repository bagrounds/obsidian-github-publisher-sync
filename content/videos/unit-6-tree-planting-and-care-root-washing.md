# 🌳🚿🪴 Unit 6: Tree Planting and Care- Root Washing  
![Unit 6: Tree Planting and Care- Root Washing](https://youtu.be/hvnkBifvIyU)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
* 🚿 Root washing uses water to remove nursery mulch and expose hidden structural issues before planting \[[00:14](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=14)].  
* 🎯 Concentrated hose nozzles act like a spatula to strip away material and reveal the root collar \[[01:43](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=103)].  
* 😵 Girdling roots wrap around the trunk and will eventually strangle the tree if left intact \[[01:10](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=70)].  
* 👃 Find the root flare or buttress roots to ensure the tree is not planted too deep in the soil \[[03:52](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=232)].  
* 📉 Planting a tree every inch too deep reduces available oxygen to the roots by ten percent \[[04:06](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=246)].  
* ✂️ Removing circling roots is essential even if it seems drastic because they limit the trees lifespan \[[05:03](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=303)].  
* 📏 Smaller container trees often catch up to larger ones because their root systems are more balanced \[[07:54](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=474)].  
* 💧 Avoid daily watering to force the tree to grow expansive roots in search of moisture \[[11:24](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=684)].  
* ⏲️ Weekly deep watering of about twenty gallons is ideal for establishing fifteen gallon trees \[[10:57](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=657)].  
* 📟 Use a moisture meter to distinguish between signs of overwatering and drought stress \[[10:34](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=634)].  
  
## 🤔 Evaluation  
* 🌲 The video emphasizes aggressive root pruning for container stock which aligns with modern arboricultural standards like those in Best Management Practices: Tree Planting by the International Society of Arboriculture.  
* ⚖️ While the speaker advocates for bare rooting, some traditional views suggest keeping the root ball intact to avoid transplant shock. However, research from the University of Florida’s Landscape Plants department supports the removal of exterior soil to fix defects.  
* 🔍 Further exploration into species-specific tolerance for root washing would enhance understanding as some sensitive species may react poorly to total soil removal.  
  
## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🪱 Q: Why should I wash the soil off a new tree before planting it?  
🧼 A: Washing exposes girdling roots and the root flare which are often buried too deep in nursery pots and can cause premature tree death if not corrected \[[01:10](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=70)].  
  
### 🛠️ Q: What tools are needed to perform a proper root wash?  
🔫 A: A standard garden hose with a high pressure concentrated nozzle and a pair of sharp bypass pruners to remove circling roots are the primary tools required \[[00:40](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=40)].  
  
### 🧱 Q: How deep should a tree be placed in its new planting hole?  
🌳 A: The structural roots or root flare should be at or slightly above the soil surface to ensure the tree receives adequate oxygen \[[03:52](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=232)].  
  
### 🌊 Q: Is it harmful to water a newly planted tree every single day?  
🚫 A: Yes because daily watering makes the tree a water pig that fails to grow an expansive root system necessary for long term stability \[[11:24](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hvnkBifvIyU&t=684)].
