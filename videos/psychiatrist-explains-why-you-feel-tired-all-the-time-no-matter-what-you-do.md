# 😴🧠👨‍⚕️ Psychiatrist Explains Why You Feel Tired All The Time (No Matter What You Do...)  
![Psychiatrist Explains Why You Feel Tired All The Time (No Matter What You Do...)](https://youtu.be/0sppw7Zq35w)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
  
* 🔋 Tiredness is often an emotion and a mental state rather than just a physiological lack of energy or sleep \[[00:13](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0sppw7Zq35w&t=13)].  
* 🧠 Feelings of exhaustion serve as an adaptive mechanism designed by the brain to prevent the pursuit of low-success, high-workload tasks \[[04:58](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0sppw7Zq35w&t=298)].  
* 🛑 The brain flips the tired switch to discourage investing energy in long-term goals with questionable outcomes or invisible progress \[[07:27](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0sppw7Zq35w&t=447)].  
* 📉 Modern obligations like taxes or dissertations trigger this ancient conservation strategy because the brain perceives the workload as overwhelming and never-ending \[[08:02](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0sppw7Zq35w&t=482)].  
* 📞 Sudden onset fatigue, such as feeling drained upon seeing a specific phone call, indicates a psychological boundary or an anticipation of emotional depletion \[[02:50](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0sppw7Zq35w&t=170)].  
* 🔍 To overcome this state, identify the specific scenario causing the fatigue and ask what the body is trying to avoid \[[13:15](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0sppw7Zq35w&t=795)].  
* 📝 Breaking large, abstract tasks into small, achievable chunks prevents the brain from viewing the effort as a waste of energy \[[16:33](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0sppw7Zq35w&t=993)].  
* 🎯 Evaluate how you will feel after completing a task; if the brain predicts feeling worse or making no progress, it will continue to induce tiredness \[[15:42](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0sppw7Zq35w&t=942)].  
  
## 🤔 Evaluation  
  
* 🔬 While the speaker frames tiredness as an emotion, the Mayo Clinic defines fatigue as a lingering tiredness that is often a symptom of underlying medical conditions or lifestyle factors like diet and sedentary behavior.  
* 📋 The concept of the brain disincentivizing high-effort tasks aligns with the principle of Least Effort in cognitive psychology, as discussed in [🤔🐇🐢 Thinking, Fast and Slow](../books/thinking-fast-and-slow.md) by Daniel Kahneman and published by Farrar, Straus and Giroux.  
* 🧬 Areas for further exploration include the intersection of chronic fatigue syndrome and emotional exhaustion to distinguish between clinical pathology and psychological defense mechanisms.  
  
## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🥱 Q: Why do I feel suddenly exhausted when looking at my to-do list?  
  
🥱 A: This happens because your brain performs a cost-benefit analysis and perceives the total workload as too high for the likely immediate reward, triggering a tired response to conserve your energy.  
  
### 🛑 Q: Is it possible for tiredness to be a boundary rather than a lack of sleep?  
  
🛑 A: Yes, tiredness can function as a psychological signal that a specific interaction or task is perceived as a waste of energy or emotionally draining, effectively acting as a defense mechanism to keep you from engaging.  
  
### 🛠️ Q: How can I stop feeling tired when I have a long-term project to finish?  
  
🛠️ A: You should divide the project into tiny, manageable pieces so your brain sees a clear path to completion for each segment, which prevents it from triggering the conservation reflex associated with overwhelming tasks.
