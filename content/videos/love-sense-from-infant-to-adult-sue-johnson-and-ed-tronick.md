# 👶❤️👩‍❤️‍👨 Love Sense: from Infant to Adult (Sue Johnson and Ed Tronick)  
![Love Sense: from Infant to Adult (Sue Johnson and Ed Tronick)](https://youtu.be/OyCHT9AbD_Y)  
  
## 🤖 AI Summary  
  
* 🧬 Bonding serves as the primary survival strategy for humans from birth until death.  
* 💃 The emotional dance of bonding consists of five core moves: reaching for connection, protesting when ignored, shutting down to protect oneself, experiencing emotional meltdown, and finding a path to repair.  
* 👶 A lack of emotional response causes infants to experience immediate stress, evidenced by negative emotions, behavioral shifts, and loss of postural control.  
* 👫 Adults in relationships process emotional disconnection similarly to infants, triggering internal alarms that interpret the lack of responsiveness as a significant safety threat.  
* 💔 Failure to respond to a partner's emotional call creates a pattern of desperate reaching or defensive withdrawal, which damages relationship security.  
* 🔄 Relationship repair occurs when partners successfully tune into each other's distress, validate vulnerability, and provide emotional presence, effectively calming the nervous system.  
  
## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🧩 Q: What are the primary stages of the emotional bonding dance?  
  
A: The emotional bonding dance involves five stages, starting with the initial attempt to reach or invite connection \[[00:39](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=39)]. When an expected response fails to occur, individuals typically enter a stage of protest, followed by a defensive phase of turning away or shutting down \[[00:50](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=50)]. These pressures lead to a final stage of emotional meltdown, which can only be resolved through a successful turn back toward the partner to initiate repair \[[01:00](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=60)].  
  
### 🔒 Q: Why does emotional disconnection cause such intense physical and mental reactions?  
  
A: Emotional disconnection acts as a fundamental danger cue because humans rely on proximity to protective loved ones as a core survival mechanism \[[00:20](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=20)]. When this connection is severed, the brain processes the lack of responsiveness as a loss of safety and comfort \[[06:47](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=407)]. This triggers an automatic, overwhelming panic response, which can cause both infants and adults to lose their ability to regulate emotions \[[06:56](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=416)].  
  
### 🤝 Q: What defines the difference between a lasting relationship and one prone to conflict?  
  
A: The differentiator between lasting love and constant conflict is the ability to successfully initiate and complete the process of repair \[[08:52](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=532)]. While all relationships experience painful moments of disconnection, couples who survive and thrive are those who can move out of the resulting aloneness \[[09:01](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=541)]. This requires one partner to tune into the other's distress on an emotional level and respond with genuine presence to calm the nervous system \[[09:18](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyCHT9AbD_Y&t=558)].
