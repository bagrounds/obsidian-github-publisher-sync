## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🍎 Q: What is 🥕 the core concept of intensive gardening on a small plot?  
🍎 A: The core concept involves 📏 maximizing yield by planting crops much closer together than in traditional rows, utilizing vertical space with trellises, and employing succession planting to keep the soil productive year-round, which allows one eighth of an acre to feed a large family.  
  
### 🧅 Q: Why should 🥔 potatoes and onions never be stored together?  
🧅 A: Potatoes and onions must 🚫 be stored separately because onions release ethylene gas and moisture, which causes potatoes to sprout prematurely and rot much faster. 🌡️ They also have conflicting ideal storage conditions: potatoes need high humidity, while onions require a dry environment.  
  
### 🏡 Q: What are the 🔑 key components for building a successful basement cold room?  
🏡 A: A successful cold room must be 🧱 insulated from the main basement area, ideally built against two exterior earth-touching walls, and requires good 💨 ventilation, often with adjustable dampers, to control temperature, humidity, and prevent the buildup of gasses that cause produce to spoil.  
  
### 🥕 Q: How can ⏰ root vegetable storage simplify food preservation?  
🥕 A: Storing large amounts of 🥒 root vegetables simplifies preservation because crops like carrots and potatoes require minimal processing, unlike fruits or leafy greens. They can be stored for months in a cold room or in sealed bags in a refrigerator, eliminating the time and equipment needed for canning or freezing.
