## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🧽 Q: Why should I wash the soil off the roots of a new plant?  
🧼 A: Removing the nursery media allows you to see and correct circling or kinked roots that would otherwise eventually choke and kill the plant as it grows.  
  
### 🏜️ Q: Is it wrong to add compost to the hole when planting a tree?  
🛑 A: Yes, because creating a pocket of rich soil surrounded by heavy native clay creates a physical interface that traps water and discourages roots from spreading into the surrounding landscape.  
  
### 🪵 Q: What is the best way to apply mulch around a new shrub?  
📏 A: Apply a four-inch layer of coarse wood chips over the root zone but taper the thickness down to zero as you reach the trunk to prevent moisture-related rot and pest damage.  
  
### ✂️ Q: Should I prune the top of the plant to balance out root loss?  
❌ A: No, you should leave the top intact because the leaves produce the energy and hormones necessary for the plant to grow new roots and recover from transplanting.
