## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)  
  
### 🗳️ Q: What is the primary role of the House Administration Committee in elections?  
🛡️ A: This committee oversees the management of federal elections and serves as the final arbiter for contested House seats, ensuring the integrity of the seating process for members.  
  
### 💰 Q: How does the Appropriations Committee differ from the Ways and Means Committee?  
💸 A: Appropriations decides how much money is spent on specific programs, while Ways and Means focuses on generating revenue through tax policy to fund those expenditures.  
  
### 📜 Q: What are the proposed ethics reforms for the United States Supreme Court?  
⚖️ A: Proposals include establishing a formal code of ethics and implementing a system where justices move to senior status after 18 years to ensure regular appointments.  
  
### 🚫 Q: Is it legal for federal agents to be present at polling stations during an election?  
🚔 A: No, it is a violation of federal law carrying criminal penalties for armed federal agents to be at polling places, as this is legally classified as voter intimidation.
